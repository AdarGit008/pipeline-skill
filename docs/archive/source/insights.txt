**[Development Workflow]** Most enterprise teams cannot adopt trunk-based development because they lack a sufficiently robust automated test suite and rely on manual QA/product-owner verification, forcing a multi-branch strategy (Dev → Staging → Prod) where each branch triggers a full environment deployment.
**[Infrastructure]** Infrastructure as Code (IaC) is essential not just for automation but to guarantee identical environments across Dev, Staging, and Prod; it prevents configuration drift and lets developers spin up completely isolated experimental environments on demand without clicking in the AWS console.
**[Compliance]** Multi-region high-availability is far more complex than simple data replication—data sovereignty laws (e.g., EU to US) often forbid moving user data across regions, making truly global active-active architectures a legal minefield.
**[Incident Response]** Feature flags serve as an immediate kill-switch for new bugs in production, allowing teams to disable a broken feature without a rollback or hotfix and dramatically reduce mean time to recovery.
**[Branching Strategy]** Production hotfixes branched directly off main create version divergence; they must be back-merged into Dev and Staging branches afterward, adding a coordination overhead that many teams underestimate.
